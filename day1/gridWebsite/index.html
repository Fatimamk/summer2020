Columns

Doric Columns

They have a fluted shaft but are otherwise the most plain. They are also the oldest order.

Ionic Columns

They usually have a fluted shaft and they have a volute at the top. (It looks like a scroll)

Corinthian Columns

They are the most ornate of the greek order. They have a fluted shaft and floral decoration at the top.

Tuscan Columns

The Tuscan order is so similar to the Doric that it is also called the Roman Doric. However, the shaft is never fluted.

Composite Columns

The composite order is a blend of the Corinthian and the Ionic.

It looks extremely similar to the Corinthian but the acanthus is typically more prominent.

Nevertheless, it can be very difficult to distinguish from an ordinary corinthian.
